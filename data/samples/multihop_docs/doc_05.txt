The SolarFlow battery was designed by BrightPath Solutions as a next-generation residential energy storage system intended to pair seamlessly with rooftop solar installations. Launched in mid-2023, the SolarFlow uses BrightPath's proprietary solid-state lithium battery technology, which offers significant advantages over conventional lithium-ion batteries in terms of energy density, safety, and longevity. Each SolarFlow unit has a usable capacity of 15 kilowatt-hours and can be stacked in configurations of up to four units for a total household storage capacity of 60 kilowatt-hours, enough to power an average American home for approximately two full days without any solar input.

One of the SolarFlow's key differentiators is its integrated smart management system, called EnergyIQ, which uses machine learning algorithms to optimize charging and discharging cycles based on household energy consumption patterns, weather forecasts, and utility rate schedules. This intelligent management can increase the financial return on a solar installation by up to twenty-five percent compared to passive storage systems. The SolarFlow is rated for over ten thousand charge-discharge cycles, giving it an expected operational lifespan of approximately twenty-five years with minimal degradation. The unit measures 1.1 meters tall, 0.7 meters wide, and 0.15 meters deep, and it can be wall-mounted in a garage or utility room. Priced at 8,500 dollars per unit before federal and state incentives, the SolarFlow has quickly become one of the best-selling residential batteries in the western United States, with over twelve thousand units installed in its first year on the market.
