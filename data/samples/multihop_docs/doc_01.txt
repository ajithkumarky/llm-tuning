TechVista Inc was founded in 2019 by Maria Chen in Austin, Texas. The company began as a small startup operating out of a converted warehouse in the East Austin tech corridor, with a founding team of just five engineers and a mission to make cutting-edge technology accessible to everyday consumers. Maria Chen, a former senior product manager at a major Silicon Valley firm, moved to Austin specifically to build TechVista, drawn by the city's growing tech ecosystem and lower cost of living compared to the Bay Area. Her vision was to create consumer electronics that combined premium build quality with intuitive design at price points that would not exclude middle-income households.

In its first year, TechVista secured a three-million-dollar seed round from Lone Star Ventures, an Austin-based venture capital firm that specializes in early-stage hardware companies. The funding allowed TechVista to expand its team to twenty employees and lease a proper office and prototyping lab on South Congress Avenue. By the end of 2020, the company had filed twelve patents related to display technology and power management systems. TechVista's corporate culture emphasizes rapid iteration and open collaboration, with weekly design reviews where any team member can propose new product concepts. The company's annual revenue reached fifteen million dollars by 2021, and it was recognized by the Austin Business Journal as one of the fastest-growing startups in Central Texas. TechVista currently employs over eighty people and has plans to open a second office in Denver, Colorado by mid-2025.
