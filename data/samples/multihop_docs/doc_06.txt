Sarah Martinez joined BrightPath Solutions as Chief Technology Officer in 2021, bringing with her over fifteen years of experience in energy systems engineering and battery technology development. Before joining BrightPath, she served as the director of advanced battery research at Panasonic Energy North America, where she led a team of forty researchers working on next-generation lithium-ion and solid-state battery chemistries for both automotive and stationary storage applications. She holds a Master of Science in materials science from Stanford University and a Bachelor of Science in chemical engineering from the University of California, Berkeley.

As CTO, Sarah Martinez is responsible for setting the technical direction of BrightPath's product roadmap and overseeing the company's research and development efforts. She was the driving force behind the development of the SolarFlow battery's solid-state electrolyte formulation, a breakthrough that took her team nearly two years of intensive laboratory work to achieve. Under her leadership, BrightPath's R&D department has doubled in size and the company's patent portfolio has grown by sixty percent. Martinez is a frequent speaker at industry conferences, including the International Battery Seminar and the Solar Power International expo. She has been featured in Wired, MIT Technology Review, and Bloomberg Green for her work on affordable residential energy storage. Outside the office, Sarah Martinez volunteers with Engineers Without Borders and mentors young women pursuing careers in STEM fields through a partnership with Portland Community College. She lives in the Hawthorne neighborhood of Portland with her partner and their golden retriever named Ohm.
